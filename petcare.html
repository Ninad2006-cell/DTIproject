Caring is Important

A gentle touch can cure the incurable

DO'S

Provide Proper Nutrition

Ensure a balanced and species appropriate diet. Fresh, clean water should always be available.Click the button to know which food should be given to your pet.

Regular Veterinary Check-ups

Schedule routine health check-ups and vaccinations. Treat illness and injuries promptly.

Clean Living environment

Keep their shelter clean, dry, and safe. Regularly clean food/water bowels and bedding

Social Interaction

Spend time bonding with pets(especially dogs, cats, birds).Allow herd or group animals to interact with their kind.

DON'Ts

Don't Neglect or Abuse

Never hit, yell at, or ignore animals. Avoid neglecting basic needs like food, water, and companionship.

Don't Feed Harmful Foods

Avoid toxic foods like chocolate, onions, grapes(for dogs), or dairy(for some adult animals).

Don't Overfeed

Obesity can cause health issues; follow proper feeding guidlines.

Don't Use Harsh Chemicals Around Them:

Many cleaning agents or pesticides can be toxic to animals.
