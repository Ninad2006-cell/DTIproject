Stray Dog and Cat Population

Approximately 62 million stray dogs and 9.1 million stray cats are found across India.

Social Public Visibility

77% of Indians encounter stray dogs weekly, while 68% see stray cats regularly.

Homelessness of Companion Animals:

Around 85% of companion animals in India are homeless.

Zoonotic Disease Risk

Stray animals contribute to the spread of zoonotic diseases like rabies, posing significant public health threats.

Population Control

Methods like trap-neuter-return (TNR) and contraception are used but face resource and infrastructure challenges.

Awareness and Access:

Public awareness about stray animal welfare is growing, but access to consistent veterinary care remains limited.

Welfare Issues

Many strays suffer from lack of veterinary care, contributing to their deteriorating health.

Impact on Traffic

Stray animals are often involved in traffic accidents, leading to injuries and fatalities.

Regulatory Gaps

While there are some regulations for stray animal management, enforcement is weak.

Community
